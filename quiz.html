01

Understanding Information Security

Module 1

core_definitions

  • Information Security — protecting information/resources to minimize business risk and the consequences of losing crucial data.
  • Risk — exposure to the chance of damage or loss. Threat — the source/means of an attack. Vulnerability — a weakness that exposes a system to harm.
  • Intrusion — unauthorized access/breach. Attack — a technique exploiting a vulnerability without authorization. Controls — countermeasures against threats and risk.
  • Goals: Prevention, Detection, Recovery.

cia_triad

PrincipleDefinitionMethods
ConfidentialityKeep information/communications privateEncryption, Access Controls, Steganography
IntegrityKeep information accurate & unmodifiedHashing, Digital Signatures, Certificates
AvailabilityEnsure continuous, authorized accessRedundancy, Patching

access_and_authentication

  • AAA: Authentication (verify identity) → Authorization (assign rights) → Accounting/Auditing (track & review activity).
  • Access Control Models: MAC (clearance-based), DAC (owner/ID-based), RBAC (role-based), Rule-Based (condition rules).
  • Authentication Methods: Password, Tokens, Biometrics, Geolocation, Multifactor.
Something you areSomething you have Something you knowSomewhere you are Something you do

security_practices

  • Implicit Deny — deny unless explicitly allowed.
  • Least Privilege — minimum access necessary to do the job.
  • Separation of Duties — no one person holds too much power.
  • Job Rotation & Mandatory Vacation — limit long-term unchecked access; enable audits.
02

Information Security Management Systems

Module 2

iso_27001

  • ISO/IEC 27001 (2005): a model for establishing, implementing, operating, monitoring, reviewing, maintaining, and improving an ISMS.
  • Process Approach (PDCA): Understand requirements → Implement controls → Monitor & review → Continual improvement.
  • ISMS: a systematic, organization-wide approach to managing sensitive information across people, process, and IT.
03

Security Threats and Vulnerabilities

Module 3

social_engineering

  • Phishing — fraudulent messages impersonating a legitimate source.
  • Pretexting — inventing a false scenario to obtain info.
  • Baiting — leaving infected media for a victim to use.
  • Tailgating — following an authorized person into a restricted area.
  • Shoulder Surfing — observing credentials as they're entered.

The Art of Protecting Secrets

Chapter 4

cryptography

  • Symmetric — single shared key, fast, bulk encryption.
  • Asymmetric — public/private key pair; key exchange + signatures.
  • Hashing — one-way digest used to verify integrity.
09

The Art of Ensuring Integrity

Chapter 5

integrity_tools

  • Digital Signatures — verify sender authenticity + message integrity (asymmetric crypto).
  • Certificates — issued by a CA, binding a public key to an identity (PKI).
  • Database Integrity (ACID): Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability.

The Five Nines Concept

Chapter 6

availability

99.999% availability ≈ 5 minutes downtime/year — achieved via redundancy, fault tolerance, automatic failover.

incident_response

Preparation → Identification → Containment → Eradication → Recovery → Lessons Learned.

disaster_recovery

RTO (Recovery Time Objective) and RPO (Recovery Point Objective) guide hot/warm/cold backup site planning.
